Community-led nutrition monitoring, powered by fieldwork and data science.

I am a postdoctoral researcher in International Relations / Political Science at the Geneva Graduate Institute. My research combines fieldwork, culturally adapted smartphone surveys, and data science to advance community-led monitoring of child nutrition and evidence-based decision-making in times of crisis.

I completed my PhD at the Graduate Institute, examining the social and behavioral roots of child undernutrition in Kenya. I currently work on the SNSF- and Innosuisse-funded Artificial Intelligence for Monitoring Malnutrition (AIMM) project, developing and testing an AI-enhanced smartphone app for accurate, timely, and non-intrusive nutrition monitoring in low-resource settings.
