Community-led nutrition monitoring, powered by fieldwork and data science.

I am a postdoctoral researcher in International Relations / Political Science at the Geneva Graduate Institute. My research combines fieldwork, intuitive and culturally adapted smartphone surveys, and data science to advance community-led monitoring of child nutrition and evidence-based decision-making in times of crisis.

I completed my PhD at the Institute, examining the social and behavioral roots of child undernutrition in Kenya. I currently work on the SNSF- and Innosuisse-funded Artificial Intelligence for Monitoring Malnutrition (AIMM), developing and testing an AI-enhanced smartphone app for accurate, timely, and non-intrusive nutrition monitoring in low-resource settings.

Research

Projects

  • Artificial Intelligence for Monitoring Malnutrition (AIMM) — SNSF & Innosuisse (48 months).
    Developing an AI-enhanced, household-operated smartphone tool for real-time nutrition classification using 2D and infrared images.
  • Data-to-Analysis (D2A) — SNSF Spark (Apr 2021 – Dec 2023).
    Developed a smartphone application for monitoring child nutrition in crisis-affected settings; led and evaluated a seven-month feasibility study with 180 households in Kenya; ensured GDPR-compliant data protection and quality assurance.
  • Moulding Early Risk Indicators to Anticipate Malnutrition (MERIAM) — FCDO (Sep 2019 – Mar 2021).
    Developed agent-based models (ABM) for early warning of malnutrition crises in East Africa; monitored spatio-temporal dynamics across five regions; produced analyses, reports, and interactive maps for policy decision-making.
