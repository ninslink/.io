Community-led nutrition monitoring, powered by fieldwork and data science.

I am a postdoctoral researcher in International Relations / Political Science at the Geneva Graduate Institute. My research combines fieldwork, intuitive and culturally adapted smartphone surveys, and data science to advance community-led monitoring of child nutrition and evidence-based decision-making in times of crisis.

I completed my PhD at the Institute, examining the social and behavioral roots of child undernutrition in Kenya. I currently work on the SNSF- and Innosuisse-funded Artificial Intelligence for Monitoring Malnutrition (AIMM), developing and testing an AI-enhanced smartphone app for accurate, timely, and non-intrusive nutrition monitoring in low-resource settings.

Research project

Artificial Intelligence for Monitoring Malnutrition (AIMM)

Funded by SNSF & Innosuisse · Duration: 48 months

AIMM develops a low-cost, household-operated tool for monitoring child undernutrition using image-based AI, enabling real-time classification without physical scales or measuring tapes.

  • Accuracy: Deep learning models estimate weight, height, MUAC, and WHZ from images.
  • Low cost: Replaces expensive, infrequent surveys with real-time monitoring.
  • Empowerment: Caregivers self-monitor children using a non-invasive mobile app.
  • Adaptability: Open-source design enables uptake across health systems.
