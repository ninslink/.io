Community-led nutrition monitoring, powered by fieldwork and data science.

I am a postdoctoral researcher in International Relations / Political Science at the Geneva Graduate Institute. My research combines fieldwork, intuitive and culturally adapted smartphone surveys, and data science to advance community-led monitoring of child nutrition and evidence-based decision-making in times of crisis.

I completed my PhD at the Institute, examining the social and behavioral roots of child undernutrition in Kenya. I currently work on the SNSF- and Innosuisse-funded Artificial Intelligence for Monitoring Malnutrition (AIMM) project, developing and testing an AI-enhanced smartphone app for accurate, timely, and non-intrusive nutrition monitoring in low-resource settings.

Research project

Artificial Intelligence for Monitoring Malnutrition (AIMM)

Funded by the Swiss National Science Foundation (SNSF) and Innosuisse · Duration: 48 months

Accelerating progress toward Sustainable Development Goal 2 (SDG 2)—ending global hunger and undernutrition— requires granular, low-cost, and near-real-time data on children’s nutritional status. Existing data systems remain fragmented, infrequent, and costly, particularly in crises and remote areas.

AIMM introduces a low-cost, household-operated tool for monitoring undernutrition using image-based artificial intelligence for real-time nutrition classification—without the need for physical scales or measuring tapes.

Implemented in Maharashtra, India, in collaboration with the Center for Artificial Intelligence (CAI) at FLAME University (Pune), the project pursues four core objectives:

  • Enhance accuracy: Deep learning models estimating weight, height, MUAC, and WHZ from 2D and infrared images.
  • Provide a real-time alternative: Reducing cost and logistics barriers of traditional surveys.
  • Empower caregivers: Enabling self-monitoring through a non-invasive, AI-powered mobile app.
  • Increase adaptability: Open-source design supporting uptake across contexts and health systems.

By democratizing nutrition data collection, AIMM strengthens community agency and accountability while offering a blueprint for integrating near real-time data into global early-warning and SDG monitoring systems.
